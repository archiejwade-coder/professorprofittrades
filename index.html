What is R?

“R” is the fixed amount of risk per trade. It is the distance between your entry and stop-loss, expressed as 1R.

Example: if your risk per trade is £100, then:

  • +1R = +£100 profit
  • +2R = +£200 profit
  • -1R = -£100 loss

Because every trade is measured in R, performance is comparable across different pairs, position sizes and account balances.

The equity curve on this page is the running total of R across all trades – not account balance in £.
